About Me

Hello! I'm Nick Usoff, a computer science student at Tufts University. Currently I dedicate my time to schoolwork, TAing for an intro level CS class, running on the Tufts track team, and working on side projects. I love all things comp sci, though my focus has been on back end web development recently, mainly with Node.js and Java.

When I have some free time, I also like to do things like discuss public health policy and how the technology revolution can help change the industry, or how the internet can help groups communicate and spread information. I have some ideas for big projects I'd like to take on in the future, and if you want to talk with me about things like health information exchange, technology to reduce food waste, or any topic related to health or food policy and advancement please shoot me an email, I'd love to hear from you!

Something I've gotten into more recently is data science and visualization. I've focused mainly on sports so far, but if you have any data sets you'd think would be cool to analyze let me know! Check out my blog for some of my data driven journalism pieces

Reasonable Care 0.2

A web app to find a health care provider in Massachusetts

The revamped Reasonable Care site, his time focusing on finding a health care provider. Angular.js is used extensively to rank health care plans on the Massachusetts health exchange, and give the user the best heatlh care plan for their needs.

Try it!

P2P Text

I spent this past summer trying to tackle the task of creating a textbook exchange for students at Tufts. I created a system where students could post textbooks at a price of their choosing, search for textbooks by class or by name, and interact with people they are buying or selling a textbook with. Though my timing was a bit off and it never really caught on, I still learned a lot about Node.js, MongoDB, Angular.js, and the Express framework

Check it out!

Reasonable Care 0.1

Reasonable Care started at the Fall 2014 Tufts Hackathon, as an application to help people without health insurance find a hospital to go to based on which condition they had as well as which quality metrics they care about. The app ended up winning the "Hack for Good" prize at the hackathon, and I got a lot of good feedback! It is built in Angular.js

Try it!
